Personality disorders are a group of mental health conditions characterized by persistent patterns of thoughts, feelings, and behaviors that deviate from cultural expectations and cause significant distress or impairment in daily functioning.

There are several types of personality disorders, including:

    Borderline Personality Disorder: involves intense and unstable emotions, self-image, and relationships.

    Narcissistic Personality Disorder: involves a grandiose sense of self-importance, a lack of empathy, and a need for admiration.

    Antisocial Personality Disorder: involves a disregard for the rights of others, impulsivity, and a lack of remorse.

    Obsessive-Compulsive Personality Disorder: involves a preoccupation with order, perfectionism, and control.
