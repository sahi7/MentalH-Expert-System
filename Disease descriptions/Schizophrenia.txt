Schizophrenia is a chronic and severe mental health disorder that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. The symptoms of schizophrenia can be divided into three categories: positive symptoms, negative symptoms, and cognitive symptoms. Here are some common symptoms of schizophrenia:

Positive Symptoms:

    Hallucinations, such as hearing voices or seeing things that aren't there.
    Delusions, such as false beliefs or ideas that are not based on reality.
    Disordered thinking, which can lead to speech that is difficult to understand or follow.
    Abnormal motor behavior, such as unusual body movements or postures.

Negative Symptoms:

    Loss of interest in activities or lack of motivation.
    Reduced emotional expression or flat affect.
    Social withdrawal and isolation.
    Reduced speech output, also known as poverty of speech.

Cognitive Symptoms:

    Difficulty with executive functioning, such as planning, organizing, and prioritizing.
    Problems with working memory, which can lead to difficulty learning and remembering new information.
    Difficulty with attention and concentration.
    Impaired decision-making and problem-solving.
