Anxiety disorders are a group of mental health disorders characterized by excessive and persistent feelings of anxiety, fear, and worry. Symptoms of anxiety disorders may vary depending on the type of disorder, but some common symptoms include:

    Excessive worry: A persistent and excessive worry or fear about everyday situations, which is difficult to control.

    Panic attacks: Sudden and unexpected episodes of intense fear or discomfort, accompanied by physical symptoms such as rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, and shortness of breath.

    Agitation: Restlessness, irritability, and difficulty concentrating or sleeping.

    Avoidance behaviors: Avoiding situations or places that trigger anxiety, such as social situations or places with crowds.

    Muscle tension: Muscle tension or aches, headaches, and other physical symptoms related to anxiety.

    Obsessive thoughts or behaviors: Obsessive or compulsive behaviors, such as repeatedly checking things, or obsessive thoughts about things like germs or cleanliness.

    Phobias: An extreme fear or aversion to specific objects or situations, such as heights, spiders, or enclosed spaces.

    Generalized anxiety: Excessive and persistent worry about everyday events or activities, often accompanied by physical symptoms such as fatigue, headaches, and muscle tension.
